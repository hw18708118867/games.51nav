## About the Typing Speed Test

The Typing Speed Test is one of the most practical and widely-used cognitive tests available. Originally developed for clerical and secretarial job assessments, typing speed tests have evolved into a valuable tool for anyone looking to improve their keyboard efficiency.

### Why Typing Speed Matters

In today's digital world, typing is an essential skill. Whether you're a student taking notes, a professional writing emails, or a programmer writing code, faster and more accurate typing can save you hours each week. The average person spends 2-3 hours per day typing. Improving your WPM by just 20 words per minute could save you 30+ minutes every day.

### How Our Test Works

Our typing test presents you with a passage of text that you must type as accurately as possible. The test measures both your speed (in WPM) and your accuracy (as a percentage). Your final score is calculated as net WPM, which accounts for errors made during the test.

### What the Results Mean

- **0-30 WPM**: Below average — you would benefit from touch typing practice
- **30-45 WPM**: Average — typical for most casual computer users
- **45-60 WPM**: Above average — good for most office environments
- **60-80 WPM**: Excellent — faster than ~90% of the population
- **80-100+ WPM**: Exceptional — competitive typing speed

### Tips for Long-Term Improvement

The key to lasting improvement is consistent, deliberate practice. Rather than taking the test repeatedly in one session, spread your practice across multiple days. Your brain consolidates motor skills during sleep, so daily practice is far more effective than cramming.

Focus on accuracy over speed. Research shows that correcting errors slows you down more than typing at a slightly slower but more accurate pace. As your accuracy improves, your speed will naturally increase without conscious effort.
