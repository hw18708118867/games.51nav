## About Dual N-Back

The Dual N-Back task was developed by researchers seeking to push the boundaries of working memory training. Unlike simpler memory tasks, Dual N-Back requires you to simultaneously track two independent streams of information — a spatial position and an auditory/visual letter.

### The Controversial Study

In 2008, a study by Jaeggi and colleagues claimed that training with Dual N-Back could increase fluid intelligence — the ability to solve novel problems independently of acquired knowledge. The study generated enormous interest and controversy. While subsequent research has produced mixed results, Dual N-Back remains one of the most studied and respected cognitive training paradigms.

### How It Challenges Your Brain

Dual N-Back is demanding because it requires continuous updating of working memory. You must constantly add new information while discarding old information, all while comparing current stimuli to what you saw N steps ago. This "mental juggling" exercises the central executive component of working memory more intensely than almost any other cognitive task.
