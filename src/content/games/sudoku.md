## About Sudoku

Sudoku is one of the world's most popular logic puzzles, enjoyed by millions of people daily. Despite using numbers, Sudoku requires no math — it's purely a game of logic and deduction.

### The History of Sudoku

While the modern Sudoku puzzle was popularized in Japan in the 1980s (the name means "single number"), its roots trace back to Latin squares studied by the Swiss mathematician Leonhard Euler in the 18th century. The puzzle gained worldwide popularity in the early 2000s and is now a staple of newspapers and puzzle apps everywhere.

### Why Sudoku Is Great Brain Training

Sudoku exercises several cognitive skills simultaneously: logical reasoning, pattern recognition, working memory, and concentration. Studies have suggested that regular puzzle-solving activities like Sudoku may help maintain cognitive function and delay age-related cognitive decline.
