## About Quick Math

Quick Math flips the script on traditional arithmetic tests. Instead of asking you to calculate answers, it asks you to verify them. Is "47 + 38 = 85" correct? You need to decide — fast.

### Verification vs. Calculation

Psychologically, verifying an answer is different from generating one. When you verify, you can use estimation, rounding, and pattern recognition — skills that are often faster and more practical than exact calculation. In real life, we verify more often than we calculate: checking a restaurant bill, estimating a discount, or validating a budget.

### The Speed-Accuracy Tradeoff

Quick Math forces you to balance speed against accuracy. Go too fast and you'll make mistakes. Go too slow and you won't maximize your score. Finding the optimal balance is a metacognitive challenge — you need to know when to trust your judgment and when to double-check.
