## About Simon Says

Simon — the iconic electronic memory game — was invented by Ralph Baer and Howard J. Morrison and released in 1978. It became an instant cultural phenomenon and remains one of the most recognizable memory games ever created.

### The Original Simon

The original Simon device featured four large colored buttons (red, blue, green, yellow) that lit up and produced distinct tones. Players had to repeat increasingly long sequences. The game was revolutionary for its time — combining memory testing with electronic feedback in a way that was both challenging and addictive.

### Why It Endures

Simon's enduring appeal lies in its simplicity. The rules can be explained in seconds, but the challenge scales infinitely. Each successful round adds one more step, making the game progressively harder. This "easy to learn, impossible to master" quality is the hallmark of great game design.
