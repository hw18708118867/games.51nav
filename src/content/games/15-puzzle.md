## About the 15 Puzzle

The 15 Puzzle was invented in 1874 by Noyes Palmer Chapman and became a worldwide craze in 1880. People carried small versions everywhere, and $1,000 prizes were offered for solutions. The puzzle's mathematical properties — half of all configurations are unsolvable — fascinated mathematicians for decades.

### The Sliding Puzzle Legacy

The 15 Puzzle spawned an entire genre of sliding puzzles. Its simple mechanism — one empty space, 15 numbered tiles — creates billions of possible configurations. The challenge of finding the shortest solution path exercises spatial reasoning, planning, and systematic thinking.
