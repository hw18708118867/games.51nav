## About the Reaction Time Test

The Reaction Time Test is a simple but powerful tool for measuring your visual-motor response speed. It's based on the same principles used in cognitive psychology research and sports science to measure processing speed.

### The Science of Reaction Time

Reaction time is the interval between the presentation of a stimulus and your response to it. It involves several stages of neural processing: your eyes detect the color change, this signal travels to your brain's visual cortex, your brain processes the information and decides to act, and finally a motor signal is sent to your finger to click.

This entire process typically takes 200-250 milliseconds in healthy adults. The fastest possible human reaction time is around 100-120ms, limited by the speed of neural transmission.

### What Your Results Mean

- **100-150ms**: Exceptional — competitive gamer or elite athlete level
- **150-200ms**: Excellent — well above average
- **200-250ms**: Average — typical for healthy adults
- **250-300ms**: Below average — may improve with practice
- **300ms+**: Slow — could indicate fatigue or distraction

### Why Reaction Time Matters

Quick reaction time is crucial in many real-world situations, from driving safely to excelling in sports and video games. Regular testing can help you monitor your cognitive health — a sudden decline in reaction time could indicate fatigue, stress, or other health issues worth addressing.

### How to Get the Most Accurate Results

For the most consistent measurements, take the test at the same time each day, in the same environment. Use a mouse rather than a touchpad for more precise timing. Avoid testing when you're tired, hungry, or distracted. Your first few attempts may be slower as you get used to the test — this is normal.
