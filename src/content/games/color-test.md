## About the Color Perception Test

The Color Perception Test challenges your ability to discriminate between similar colors. This skill, known as color discrimination or hue discrimination, is an important aspect of visual perception that varies significantly between individuals.

### The Science of Color Vision

Human color vision is made possible by cone cells in the retina — photoreceptors sensitive to different wavelengths of light. Most people have three types of cones (trichromats), sensitive to short (blue), medium (green), and long (red) wavelengths.

The relative sensitivity of these cones varies between individuals, which is why some people are better at distinguishing certain color ranges than others. Women typically have better color discrimination than men, and some women may even have four types of cones (tetrachromats), giving them exceptional color vision.

### How Our Test Works

Each round presents you with a target color and four similar options. The options are created by slightly varying the hue, saturation, and lightness of the target color. Only one option matches exactly — the others are subtly different.

The test becomes more challenging as the similar colors get closer to the target. Scoring well requires careful attention to subtle color differences.

### Why Color Perception Matters

Good color discrimination is valuable in many professions and activities. Graphic designers, photographers, painters, and interior designers all rely on precise color perception. Even in everyday life, being able to distinguish subtle color differences helps with everything from choosing ripe produce to coordinating outfits.

### Improving Your Color Perception

Like many perceptual skills, color discrimination can improve with practice. Spending time consciously comparing colors, learning color theory, and regularly challenging yourself with tests like this one can sharpen your color acuity over time. Many artists report that their color perception improved significantly during their training.
