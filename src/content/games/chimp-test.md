## About the Chimp Memory Test

The Chimp Test is inspired by one of the most famous experiments in cognitive psychology. In 2007, researchers at Kyoto University's Primate Research Institute demonstrated that young chimpanzees possessed extraordinary working memory for spatial-numerical associations.

### The Famous Study

In the original experiment, chimpanzees were shown numbers 1-9 briefly displayed on a touchscreen. After the numbers disappeared (replaced by white squares), the chimps had to touch the squares in numerical order. Remarkably, the chimps could remember all 9 positions with high accuracy, even when the numbers were displayed for just 210 milliseconds.

### Human vs. Chimp Performance

When the same test was given to human adults, performance was significantly worse. Humans typically struggled to remember more than 5-6 positions. This finding challenged assumptions about human cognitive superiority and highlighted the incredible working memory capabilities of our primate cousins.

### What This Test Reveals

The Chimp Test measures the capacity and precision of your visuospatial working memory — the ability to briefly hold and manipulate visual information. It's a purer measure of working memory than many other tests because it relies minimally on language or learned strategies.
