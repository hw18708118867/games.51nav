## About Mental Rotation

Mental rotation is one of the most studied abilities in cognitive psychology. Pioneering research by Shepard and Metzler in 1971 showed that people mentally rotate objects at a roughly constant speed — the more rotation required, the longer it takes to decide if two shapes match.

### The Classic Experiment

Shepard and Metzler showed participants pairs of 3D block figures and asked if they were the same shape. The key finding: response time increased linearly with the angle of rotation between the two shapes. This suggested that people were performing an analog mental rotation — imagining the shape turning in their mind — rather than using a purely analytical strategy.

### Why It's Important

Spatial reasoning — including mental rotation — is a distinct form of intelligence that predicts success in STEM fields. Engineers, surgeons, pilots, and architects all rely heavily on spatial reasoning skills. Research has shown that spatial ability is a better predictor of STEM achievement than math or verbal ability alone.
