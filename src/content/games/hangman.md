## About Hangman

Hangman is one of the oldest and most widely played word games in the world. Its origins date back to Victorian England, where it was known as "Birds, Beasts, and Fishes." The game has been a classroom staple for generations, used by teachers to make vocabulary practice fun.

### The Classic Formula

The premise is simple: guess letters to reveal a hidden word. Each wrong guess brings the hangman closer to completion. The tension builds with each wrong letter, creating a compelling risk-reward dynamic. The game is universally understood and requires no complex rules — just a vocabulary and a willingness to guess.

### Cognitive Benefits

Hangman exercises multiple cognitive skills: vocabulary recall, pattern recognition, probabilistic reasoning, and working memory. Players must track which letters have been used, consider letter frequency, and use contextual hints to narrow down possibilities. It's a deceptively rich cognitive exercise disguised as a simple game.
